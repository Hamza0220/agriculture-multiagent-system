DISEASE: Yellow Rust (Puccinia striiformis) — "Zard Zang"
=========================================================================
Crop: wheat (gandum) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Rabi
Region: Punjab (severe), KPK | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Yellow to orange-yellow stripes (pustules) running parallel to leaf veins
- Yellow powder (urediniospores) rubs off on fingers when touched
- First appears on lower leaves, spreads upward
- In severe cases, leaves turn completely yellow and die
- Disease develops rapidly at 10-15°C with high humidity (>80%)

Conditions Favoring Disease:
- Cool temperatures (10-15°C) with frequent rain or dew
- Early sown wheat (October) more susceptible
- Dense canopy with poor air circulation
- Continuous wheat cropping without rotation
- Susceptible varieties (lack of resistant genes)

Chemical Treatment:
1. Propiconazole 25EC (Tilt 250EC — Syngenta) — 200ml per acre in 100L water
   Cost: Rs. 800-1200 per acre
   Apply at first appearance of symptoms
   Second spray after 10-14 days if needed
   Local brands: Tilt 250EC (Syngenta), Bumper 25EC

2. Azoxystrobin + Difenoconazole (Amistar Top, Rekado) — 200ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 1000-1500 per acre

3. Triadimefon — 100g per acre
   Cost: Rs. 400-600 per acre
   [VERIFY WITH LOCAL EXPERT]

4. Tebuconazole + Trifloxystrobin (Nativo) — 200ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 1200-1600 per acre

Application Guidelines:
- Spray in early morning (6-9 AM) or late evening (5-7 PM)
- Avoid spraying if rain expected within 6 hours
- Use hollow cone nozzles for better coverage
- Start spraying from field edges (disease enters from edges)
- Complete spray within 2-3 days of symptom detection

Resistant Varieties for Pakistan:
- Punjab-2011 (moderately resistant)
- Faisalabad-2008 (moderately resistant)
- Ujala-2015
- Zincol-2016
- Akbar-2019
- Very susceptible: Galaxy-2013, Sehar-2006

Prevention:
- Grow resistant varieties
- Avoid early sowing (sow after November 15)
- Remove volunteer wheat plants before new season
- Treat seed with fungicides before sowing
- Rotate with non-host crops (chickpea, lentil)

DISEASE: Leaf Rust (Puccinia triticina) — "Pati Zang" / "Bhoora Zang"
=========================================================================
Crop: wheat (gandum) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Rabi
Region: All wheat-growing regions | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Small, circular to oval orange-brown pustules on leaf surfaces
- Pustules appear scattered on both upper and lower leaf surfaces
- Yellow halo surrounds each pustule
- Leaves dry up prematurely in severe infections
- Can cause yield losses of 15-40% in susceptible varieties

Conditions Favoring Disease:
- Moderate temperatures (15-22°C)
- High humidity and frequent rainfall
- Dense crop canopy
- Late sowing increases risk

Chemical Treatment (same options as Yellow Rust):
1. Propiconazole 25EC (Tilt 250EC) — 200ml per acre
2. Azoxystrobin + Difenoconazole — 200ml per acre

Prevention:
- Resistant varieties: Punjab-2011, Faisalabad-2008, Zincol-2016
- Balanced nitrogen fertilization (excess N increases susceptibility)
- Timely sowing (November 15-December 15)

DISEASE: Stem Rust (Puccinia graminis) — "Tana Zang" / "Kala Zang"
=========================================================================
Crop: wheat (gandum) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Rabi
Region: Punjab, Sindh | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Large, elongated reddish-brown pustules on stems, leaf sheaths, and leaves
- Pustules rupture stem epidermis, giving ragged appearance
- Black teliospores develop later in the season (hence "black rust")
- Causes stem weakening, lodging, and shriveled grains
- Most damaging rust disease — can cause 100% yield loss in severe cases
- Note: Less common in Pakistan than yellow rust

Conditions Favoring Disease:
- Higher temperatures (20-30°C) with high humidity
- Late-sown crops at higher risk

Chemical Treatment:
1. Tebuconazole (Folicur) — 250ml per acre
2. Propiconazole + Tebuconazole combination products
3. Apply as soon as pustules are detected on stems

Prevention:
- Grow resistant varieties (all modern Pakistani varieties have Sr gene resistance)
- Avoid growing wheat near barberry bushes (alternate host)
- Eradicate volunteer wheat before new season

DISEASE: Powdery Mildew (Blumeria graminis) — "Safed Phaphundi"
=========================================================================
Crop: wheat (gandum) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Rabi
Region: Punjab (central/irrigated areas) | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- White to light gray powdery growth on upper leaf surfaces, stems, and spikes
- Powdery coating can rub off easily
- Affected leaves turn yellow and die prematurely
- Disease starts on lower leaves and moves upward
- Reduces photosynthesis and grain filling

Conditions Favoring Disease:
- High humidity (but no free water on leaves)
- Moderate temperatures (15-20°C)
- Dense canopy with shade
- High nitrogen fertilization (succulent growth)
- Dense sowing (high seed rate)

Chemical Treatment:
1. Propiconazole 25EC — 200ml per acre (same as for rust)
2. Triadimefon (Bayleton) — 100g per acre
3. Sulfur-based fungicides — organic option, 500g per acre
   Cost: Rs. 300-500 per acre

Prevention:
- Avoid excessive nitrogen application
- Maintain recommended seed rate (50-60kg/acre)
- Resistant varieties: Lasani-2008, Faisalabad-2008

PEST: Wheat Aphid Complex — "Gandum ki Makhiyan"
=========================================================================
Crop: wheat (gandum) | Category: PEST | Season: Rabi
Region: All regions | Urgency: MODERATE

Species:
- Sitobion avenae (Grain aphid)
- Rhopalosiphum padi (Bird cherry-oat aphid)
- Schizaphis graminum (Green bug)

Symptoms:
- Large clusters of small soft-bodied insects on leaves, stems, and heads
- Sticky honeydew excretion on leaves (promotes sooty mold)
- Leaves turn yellow, curl, and dry up
- Heads with fewer grains — shriveled and light weight
- Aphid infestation can reduce yield by 20-40%

Threshold: 5-10 aphids per tiller before flowering
Treatment warranted when: Aphids present on 50%+ tillers

Chemical Treatment:
1. Imidacloprid (Confidor) — 100ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 600-800 per acre
2. Dimethoate — 400ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 300-500 per acre
3. Lambda-cyhalothrin — 150ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 400-600 per acre

Biological Control:
- Natural predators: ladybird beetles, lacewings, syrphid flies
- Avoid broad-spectrum insecticides early in season to preserve natural enemies
- Neem extract (Azadirachtin) — 500ml per acre — mild infestation

Prevention:
- Conserve natural enemies by avoiding unnecessary sprays
- Balanced nitrogen (excess N attracts aphids)
- Timely sowing reduces infestation risk

PEST: Wheat Termite — "Deemak"
=========================================================================
Crop: wheat (gandum) | Category: PEST | Season: Rabi
Region: Punjab (thinned irrigated areas), Sindh | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Plants wilt and die in scattered patches
- Roots eaten — plant pulls up easily without resistance
- Hollow stems with soil particles inside
- Damage visible in patches rather than whole field

Chemical Treatment:
- Seed treatment with Imidacloprid (Confidor) — 5ml/kg seed before sowing
- Soil drench with Chlorpyrifos — 1 liter per acre at sowing
- Apply to field edges first (termites enter from borders)

Prevention:
- Deep summer plowing exposes termites to sun
- Remove previous crop residues
- Seed treatment is most effective

DISEASE: Wheat Blight / Root Rot Complex
=========================================================================
Crop: wheat (gandum) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Rabi
Region: All regions | Urgency: MODERATE

Causes: Fusarium spp., Bipolaris sorokiniana, Rhizoctonia solani

Symptoms:
- Seedling death / poor germination
- Brown lesions on roots and crown
- Basal stem rot near soil line
- Whiteheads (prematurely ripened heads with shriveled grains)
- Stunted plants scattered in field

Chemical Treatment:
- Seed treatment: Tebuconazole (Raxil) — 1.5ml/kg seed
- Tebuconazole + Azoxystrobin seed treatment

Prevention:
- Seed treatment before sowing
- Crop rotation (avoid wheat-wheat sequence)
- Well-prepared seedbed with good drainage
- Use certified disease-free seed

FERTILIZER: Wheat Nutrient Schedule
=========================================================================
Crop: wheat (gandum) | Category: FERTILIZER | Season: Rabi
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: PLANNED

Recommended NPK per acre:
- Nitrogen (N): 50-70 kg/acre (110-155 kg Urea)
- Phosphorus (P2O5): 25-30 kg/acre (155-190 kg SSP)
- Potash (K2O): 12-15 kg/acre (20-25 kg MOP)
- Zinc Sulfate: 8-10 kg/acre (if Zn deficient soil)

Application Timing:
1. Basal (at sowing): Full P + Full K + 1/3 N
   — DAP 1 bag (50kg) + SOP 10kg OR SSP 3 bags + MOP 10kg
2. First Top-dressing (30-40 days after sowing):
   — Urea 1 bag (50kg) — at crown root initiation stage
3. Second Top-dressing (60-70 days after sowing):
   — Urea 0.5 bag (25kg) — at stem elongation/boosting stage
   — Apply after first irrigation, when field is walkable

Fertilizer Tips:
- Apply Zinc at sowing if soil is deficient
- Foliar spray of Urea 2% (200g in 10L water) at grain filling if N deficiency seen
- Sulphur: 10-15kg per acre if growing in sandy soils
- Organic: well-rotted farmyard manure 4-5 tonnes/acre before sowing [VERIFY WITH LOCAL EXPERT]

SOIL: Wheat Soil Preparation
=========================================================================
Crop: wheat (gandum) | Category: SOIL | Season: Rabi
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: PLANNED

Soil Requirements:
- Loam / clay-loam preferred
- Good drainage essential
- pH range: 6.0-7.5 (neutral to slightly acidic)
- Organic matter: >1%

Preparation:
1. Deep plowing after rice harvest (20-25cm) with moldboard plow
2. 2-3 crosswise cultivations with cultivator
3. Leveling with laser land leveler (saves 25-30% irrigation water)
4. Rotted FYM 4-5 tonnes/acre — spread 2-3 weeks before sowing
5. Final field preparation: 2 planking for seedbed finishing

HARVESTING: Wheat Harvesting Guide
=========================================================================
Crop: wheat (gandum) | Category: HARVESTING | Season: Rabi
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: THIS SEASON

Harvest Time: April-May (depending on region and sowing time)
Telltale Signs of Maturity:
- Grains turn golden brown and hard
- Grain moisture content below 14%
- Stems turn yellow-brown
- Grains separate easily from chaff when rubbed
- Moisture test: biting a grain — should crack, not squash

Harvest Method:
- Combine harvester: Most common — Rs. 2500-3500 per acre
- Manual harvesting: sickle cutting — 15-20 person-days per acre
- Best time: early morning (grain less likely to shatter)

Post-Harvest:
- Immediate threshing if manually harvested
- Sun drying: spread grains 2 inches thick, turn every hour for 3-4 days
- Safe storage moisture: below 12%
- Store in clean gunny bags or metal bins
- Use aluminum phosphide (Phostoxin) tablets for storage pest control — 1 tablet per 100kg grain
- Recent price (2025): Rs. 2400-2800 per 40kg

IRRIGATION: Wheat Irrigation Schedule
Crop: wheat (gandum) | Category: IRRIGATION | Season: Rabi
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: PLANNED
=========================================================================
Number of irrigations: 4-6 depending on soil type and rainfall

Critical Stages (do NOT miss):
1. Crown Root Initiation — 21-25 days after sowing (DAS)
2. Tillering — 45-50 DAS
3. Stem Elongation/Boosting — 70-75 DAS
4. Grain Filling — 90-100 DAS
5. Dough Stage — 110-120 DAS (optional, avoid if crop is healthy)

Schedule for Punjab (sandy loam):
- 1st irrigation: 20-25 DAS (crown root initiation) — CRITICAL
- 2nd irrigation: 45-50 DAS (tillering)
- 3rd irrigation: 70-75 DAS (stem elongation/boosting)
- 4th irrigation: 90-95 DAS (grain filling)
- 5th irrigation: 110-120 DAS (dough stage, light irrigation only)

Water depth: 2-3 inches per irrigation
Tube well hours: 3-4 hours per irrigation (for 1 acre, 1 cusec pump)
Sandier soils need more frequent but lighter irrigations

Water-saving tips:
- Laser leveling saves 25-30% water
- Skip last irrigation if crop looks healthy
- Early morning irrigation reduces evaporation loss
- Direct seeded wheat needs lighter, more frequent irrigation than transplanted
