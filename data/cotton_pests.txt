PEST: Cotton Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci) — "Safed Makhi"
=========================================================================
Crop: cotton (kapas) | Category: PEST | Season: Kharif
Region: Punjab (major), Sindh | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Small white flying insects on underside of leaves
- Leaves become sticky with honeydew
- Sooty mold fungus grows on honeydew — leaves turn black
- Leaves curl, yellow, and drop prematurely
- Heavy infestation: complete defoliation, boll drop
- Also transmits Cotton Leaf Curl Virus (CLCV) — devastating disease

Economic Threshold: 5-8 adults per leaf OR 50% leaves with at least one adult

Chemical Treatment (in rotation — whitefly develops resistance quickly):
1. Pyriproxyfen — 250ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 700-900 per acre
   Effective on eggs/nymphs, safe for predators
   
2. Buprofezin (Admiral) — 300ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 600-800 per acre
   
3. Imidacloprid (Confidor) — 100ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 600-800 per acre
   Note: WIDELY RESISTANT in many areas — do not rely on alone

4. Spirotetramat (Movento) — 150ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 1000-1200 per acre
   Very effective — works systemically from roots to leaves

5. Diafenthiuron — 300ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 500-700 per acre

6. Neem Oil — 500ml per acre (organic option)
   Use as preventative, NOT for heavy infestations

Spray Rotation Strategy:
- DO NOT use same insecticide class more than once per season
- Rotate between: IGR (Pyriproxyfen) → Neonicotinoid → Organophosphate → Oil/Soap
- Threshold-based spraying — do NOT spray on calendar schedule

Prevention:
- Avoid early planting (before March 15 in Punjab — higher whitefly risk)
- Remove alternative hosts: brinjal, okra, tobacco near cotton fields
- Use border crop: maize or sorghum around field edge
- Avoid excessive nitrogen — attracts whiteflies
- Conserve natural predators: lacewings, ladybird beetles, spiders
- Scout field twice weekly — check underside of top 5 leaves

PEST: Cotton Pink Bollworm (Pectinophora gossypiella) — "Gulabi Sundi"
=========================================================================
Crop: cotton (kapas) | Category: PEST | Season: Kharif
Region: Punjab, Sindh | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Larvae bore into flower buds (squares) and young bolls
- Damaged squares: rosette flowering (flower doesn't open properly)
- Bolls don't open fully or open with damaged lint
- Larvae inside bolls feeding on seeds and lint
- Entry/exit holes on bolls
- Yield loss can be 20-40%

Lifecycle:
- Eggs laid on leaves, squares, and bolls
- Larva enters boll within 1 hour of hatching (once inside, chemical control useless)
- Period of greatest risk: boll formation through boll opening

Chemical Treatment (timing CRITICAL — must hit before larva enters boll):
1. Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen) — 60ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 800-1000 per acre
   Best timing: at peak flowering/early boll formation
   
2. Spinosad (Tracer) — 60ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 600-800 per acre
   
3. Lambda-cyhalothrin — 200ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 400-600 per acre

Prevention:
- Bt cotton (already resistant) — but resistance is breaking in some areas
- After harvest: remove and destroy cotton stalks before March
- Deep plowing after harvest
- Close monitoring during boll formation stage
- Pheromone traps: 5-8 per acre for monitoring

PEST: Cotton Thrips / Jassids — "Naram Makhi"
=========================================================================
Crop: cotton (kapas) | Category: PEST | Season: Kharif
Region: All regions | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms (Jassid):
- Leaves turn yellow from edges inward ("hopper burn" look)
- Leaves curl downward
- Stunted plant growth
- Small green wedge-shaped insects jump when leaves disturbed

Symptoms (Thrips):
- Silver or brown patches on leaves
- Leaves curl upward
- Terminal growth damaged

Chemical Treatment:
1. Imidacloprid — 100ml per acre (jassid)
2. Spinosad — 60ml per acre (thrips)
3. Dimethoate — 400ml per acre (both)

DISEASE: Cotton Leaf Curl Virus (CLCV) — "Kapas ka Patti Murh"
=========================================================================
Crop: cotton (kapas) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: Punjab (major), Sindh | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Leaf curling upward or downward
- Thickened, darkened veins (vein thickening)
- Small leaf-like enations (outgrowths) on underside of leaves
- Stunted plant growth with reduced fruiting
- Boll shedding
- Transmitted by whitefly (Bemisia tabaci)

Management (NO CURE — only prevention):
- Grow resistant/tolerant varieties:
  - MNH-886, MNH-992, IUB-222, VH-305
  - Tarzan-1, Cyto-124
- Control whitefly population (see whitefly section)
- Remove and destroy CLCV infected plants as soon as symptoms appear
- Maintain field boundaries: don't plant near okra or tomatoes (hosts)
- Timely sowing (avoid late planting — CLCV risk increases)

Treatment for Whitefly (vector control):
Imidacloprid + Buprofezin rotation — apply at 30, 45, 60 days after emergence
Pyriproxyfen at first whitefly detection

=========================================================================
DISEASE: Cotton Root Rot (Macrophomina phaseolina) — "Jar Ki Saran"  
=========================================================================
Crop: cotton (kapas) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: Punjab, Sindh | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Sudden wilting of plants — leaves remain attached but droop
- Roots and stem base discolored brown-black
- Bark peels off easily
- Plants die in scattered patches
- Worse in hot, dry conditions

Chemical Treatment:
- Seed treatment: Carbendazim 2g/kg seed
- Soil drench: Carbendazim + Mancozeb at disease patches
- No effective cure once plants are wilting

Prevention:
- Deep summer plowing
- Crop rotation (avoid cotton-cotton)
- Add organic matter to soil
- Resistant varieties [VERIFY WITH LOCAL EXPERT]

FERTILIZER: Cotton Nutrient Schedule
=========================================================================
Crop: cotton (kapas) | Category: FERTILIZER | Season: Kharif
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: PLANNED

Recommended NPK per acre:
- Nitrogen: 55-65 kg/acre (120-140 kg Urea)
- Phosphorus: 15-20 kg/acre (95-125 kg SSP)
- Potash: 12-15 kg/acre (20-25 kg MOP)
- Zinc: 5-8 kg Zinc Sulfate per acre

Application Schedule (split application CRITICAL for cotton):
1. Basal (at sowing): Full P + Full K + 1/4 N
   — DAP 1 bag + MOP 10kg
2. First side-dressing (25-30 days after emergence): 1/4 N
   — Urea 30-35kg — at thinning/square formation
3. Second side-dressing (50-60 DAE): 1/4 N
   — Urea 30-35kg — at first flower
4. Third side-dressing (75-90 DAE): 1/4 N
   — Urea 30-35kg — at peak boll formation

CRITICAL: Cotton is sensitive to N timing. Late N → excessive vegetative growth,
more whitefly, less bolls. Stop N after peak boll formation (90 DAE).

IRRIGATION: Cotton Water Management
=========================================================================
Crop: cotton (kapas) | Category: IRRIGATION | Season: Kharif
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: PLANNED

Cotton is drought-tolerant but needs water at critical stages:

Stages:
1. Sowing to emergence: Keep soil moist (light irrigation every 5-7 days)
2. Square formation (30-50 DAE): Moderate water — 2-3 irrigations
3. Flowering & boll formation (50-90 DAE): CRITICAL — do NOT stress
   — 3-4 irrigations at 10-12 day intervals
4. Boll opening (90+ DAE): Reduce water — let field dry for harvest

Total irrigations: 6-10 depending on soil type and rainfall
Water stress at flowering → severe boll shedding

Region: Punjab | Urgency: PLANNED

Bt (approved):

3. IUB-222 — Heat tolerant
4. Tarzan-1 — CLCV resistant
5. VH-305 — Good for central Punjab
6. Cyto-124 — High ginning out turn
7. FH-152 — Good for southern Punjab
8. NIAB-878 — High yield under stress

Tips:
- Bt cotton provides protection against bollworms but NOT against whitefly/sucking pests
- Non-Bt refuge: plant 10% non-Bt cotton rows for resistance management

STORAGE: Cotton Storage
=========================================================================
Crop: cotton (kapas) | Category: STORAGE | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: PLANNED

- Pick cotton when bolls fully open (dry)
- Pick in morning after dew dries (not wet — staining reduces quality)
- Separate good quality from stained/damaged
- Store in dry, well-ventilated place
- Keep off ground (use wooden pallet)
- Recent price (2025): Rs. 5500-7000 per 40kg (seed cotton/lint varies by quality)
- Phutti (seed cotton) prices differ significantly from lint
