CROP: Mustard (sarson/raya) | Season: Rabi (Oct-Apr) | Region: Punjab, Sindh, KPK

=========================================================================
CROP: Mustard (sarson/raya/gobhi sarson) | Rabi oilseed crop
Category: GENERAL | Urgency: PLANNED

Growth Stages:
- Sowing: October-November (optimal: Oct 15-Nov 15 for Punjab)
- Germination: 5-7 days
- Vegetative growth: Nov-Dec
- Flowering: January-February
- Pod filling: February-March
- Harvest: March-April

Soil: Loam to clay loam, pH 6.0-7.5, well-drained
Seed rate: 2-3 kg/acre (drilled) or 1.5-2 kg/acre (transplanted)
Spacing: Rows 30-45cm apart

=========================================================================
DISEASE: Mustard White Rust (Albugo candida) — "Safed Zang"
Category: DISEASE | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- White shiny pustules on leaves (like white paint splatters)
- Stems may become swollen and distorted
- Flower heads may become sterile (green "staghead")
- Leaves can distort, curl, and die
- Cool, humid weather favors disease

Chemical Treatment:
1. Mancozeb (Dithane M-45, Indofil M-45) — 500g per acre in 100L water
   Cost: Rs. 300-500 per acre
   Two sprays at 15-day intervals
2. Metalaxyl + Mancozeb (Ridomil Gold) — 400g per acre (more effective)

Prevention:
- Crop rotation (avoid Brassica after Brassica)
- Remove infected plants
- Resistant varieties available [VERIFY WITH LOCAL EXPERT]
- Seed treatment with metalaxyl (1.5g per kg seed)

=========================================================================
DISEASE: Mustard Alternaria Blight (Alternaria brassicae) — "Pati Jali"
Category: DISEASE | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Brown/black target-spot lesions on leaves
- Spots coalesce in severe cases — leaves die
- Linear black spots on pods — pods split, seeds shriveled
- Yield loss: 20-40% in epidemic years
- Seed infection: causes seedling blight next year

Chemical Treatment:
1. Mancozeb — 500g per acre
   Cost: Rs. 300-500 per acre
   Apply at first appearance: 2-3 sprays at 10-15 day intervals
2. Carbendazim — 200g per acre
3. Propiconazole (Tilt) — 200ml per acre

Prevention:
- Seed treatment with Carbendazim (2g/kg seed)
- Remove and destroy crop debris
- 3-4 year crop rotation (avoid mustard after mustard)
- Balanced fertilization (excess N increases severity)

=========================================================================
DISEASE: Blackleg (Leptosphaeria maculans) — "Kala Tana"
Category: DISEASE | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Gray-green lesions at base of stem
- Lesions turn white with black dots (pycnidia)
- Stem girdled — plant wilts and dies
- Roots may rot
- Pods and seeds also infected

Note: Pakistan's Brassica species are relatively resistant to blackleg
compared to European and Australian canola

Chemical: Carbendazim seed treatment + Mancozeb foliar spray if needed

=========================================================================
PEST: Mustard Aphid (Lipaphis erysimi) — "Sarson ki Makhi"
Category: PEST | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Clusters of greenish-gray aphids on upper stem, leaves, flower buds
- Leaves curl, yellow, and become sticky
- Flowers may abort — pod set reduced
- Sooty mold on honeydew
- Can reduce yield by 40-60% if uncontrolled
- Most serious pest of mustard in Pakistan

Economic Threshold: 10-15 aphids per 10cm shoot

Chemical Treatment:
1. Imidacloprid (Confidor) — 100ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 600-800 per acre
   Apply at first cluster formation (before flowering ideally)
2. Dimethoate — 400ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 300-500 per acre
3. Lambda-cyhalothrin — 150ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 400-600 per acre
4. Neem oil — 500ml per acre — moderate control, use in early infestation

Prevention:
- Early sowing (October) helps avoid peak aphid period
- Natural predators: ladybird beetles, syrphid fly larvae — conserve them
- Border crop: mustard + wheat intercropping reduces aphid

=========================================================================
PEST: Mustard Sawfly (Athalia proxima)
Category: PEST | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Larvae feed on leaves — only veins remain (skeletonizing)
- Young plants may be killed
- Larvae: dark greenish-black, slimy appearance, 1-2cm long

Chemical Treatment:
1. Lambda-cyhalothrin — 150ml per acre
2. Quinalphos — 400ml per acre

=========================================================================
FERTILIZER: Mustard Nutrient Schedule
Category: FERTILIZER | Urgency: PLANNED

NPK per acre (for Raya and Gobhi Sarson):
- Nitrogen: 40 kg/acre (90 kg Urea)
- Phosphorus: 12 kg/acre (75 kg SSP)
- Potash: 6 kg/acre (10 kg MOP) — only if soil deficient
- Sulfur: 10-15 kg/acre — beneficial for oil content (use ammonium sulfate or SSP)

Application:
1. Basal (at sowing): Full P + Full K + Full S + 1/2 N
2. Top-dressing (30-35 DAS): 1/2 N
   — at first branching stage

Sulfur deficiency: Yellowing of young leaves, poor oil content. Mustard is
highly responsive to sulfur. Apply if using non-S containing fertilizers.

=========================================================================
IRRIGATION: Mustard Water Management
Category: IRRIGATION | Urgency: PLANNED

Total: 2-3 irrigations (more in sandy soil)

Critical stages:
1. Germination (7-10 DAS): Light irrigation
2. Flowering (45-60 DAS): CRITICAL — don't stress
3. Pod formation (70-85 DAS): CRITICAL

Mustard is drought-tolerant — but flowering and pod filling are sensitive.
Avoid waterlogging — highly sensitive.
Rainfall alone sufficient in some areas (barani/rainfed regions).

=========================================================================
HARVESTING: Mustard
Category: HARVESTING | Urgency: THIS SEASON

- Harvest when: 70-80% pods turn yellow-brown
- Cut crop close to ground with sickle
- Sun dry in bundles for 5-7 days
- Thresh by beating on hard surface or tractor
- Clean by winnowing
- Sun dry seeds for 2-3 days to <10% moisture before storage
- Market price (2025): Rs. 3000-4000 per 40kg (varies by season)

Category: VARIETIES | Urgency: PLANNED

3. KS-75 — High oil content
4. Bhawalpur Raya — Heat tolerant
5. Peela Raya — Good for mixed cropping
6. Khanpur Raya — KPK adapted
7. Toria varieties: for very early sowing (late September)
