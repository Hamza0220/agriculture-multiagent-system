CROP: Tomato (tamatar) | Season: Kharif (Jul-Dec) & Rabi (Jan-Jun)
Region: Punjab (major), Sindh, KPK | Urgency: PLANNED

DISEASE: Early Blight (Alternaria solani) — "Pati Jali"
Category: DISEASE | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Brown to black spots on lower leaves first (bullseye/target ring pattern)
- Spots enlarge, leaves turn yellow and drop
- Stem lesions and fruit rot near stem end
- Worse as plants age and fruit load increases

Chemical Treatment:
1. Mancozeb (Dithane M-45) — 500g per acre
   Cost: Rs. 300-500 per acre
   Apply at first sign, repeat every 7-10 days
2. Chlorothalonil (Bravo) — 500ml per acre
3. Copper oxychloride — 500g per acre (organic-friendly)

Prevention:
- Mulch around plants to prevent soil splash
- Stake plants for air circulation
- Remove lower infected leaves
- Avoid overhead irrigation

=========================================================================
DISEASE: Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans) — "Dair Jali"
Category: DISEASE | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Water-soaked irregular spots on leaves — rapid spread
- White fuzzy growth on leaf undersides in high humidity
- Brown firm lesions on fruit
- Plants collapse within days under favorable conditions
- Famous cause of potato famine — destroys entire fields

Conditions: Cool (15-21°C) + Wet (rain/high humidity) = explosive spread

Chemical Treatment:
1. Metalaxyl + Mancozeb (Ridomil Gold) — 400g per acre
   Cost: Rs. 600-800 per acre
   Apply preventatively when conditions favor disease
2. Chlorothalonil — 500ml per acre
3. Copper-based fungicides — moderate protection

Prevention:
- Resistant varieties available (check seed packet)
- Destroy infected plants immediately (burn/bag — don't compost)
- Don't plant near potatoes
- Stake and prune for better air flow

=========================================================================
DISEASE: Tomato Blossom End Rot (Physiological — Calcium Deficiency)
Category: DISEASE | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Water-soaked spot at blossom end of fruit
- Spot enlarges, turns brown-black, leathery
- Affects green and ripe fruit
- Secondary infection with fungi

Causes:
- Irregular watering (wet-dry cycles)
- Calcium deficiency in fruit setting stage
- High nitrogen encourages growth but calcium can't keep up

Treatment/Prevention:
- Maintain consistent soil moisture (not too wet, not too dry)
- Mulch to even out moisture
- Foliar calcium spray: Calcium nitrate 5g/liter water — 2-3 sprays weekly
- Avoid excessive nitrogen (especially ammonium-based)
- Soil test for calcium (apply gypsum if deficient)

PEST: Tomato Fruit Worm / Corn Earworm (Helicoverpa armigera) — "Phal ki Sundi"
Category: PEST | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Small holes in fruit, larvae bored inside
- Frass visible at entry hole on fruit
- Larvae feed inside fruit — fruit rots
- Terminal shoots may also be attacked

Chemical Treatment:
1. Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen) — 60ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 800-1000 per acre
   Apply at flowering/fruit set
2. Emamectin benzoate — 80g per acre
3. Spinosad — 60ml per acre

CROP: Onion (pyaaz) | Season: Rabi (Oct-May) | Region: Punjab, Sindh

DISEASE: Downy Mildew (Peronospora destructor) — "Phafhindi"
Category: DISEASE | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Pale yellow to light green spots on leaves
- Purple-gray fuzzy growth on spots in wet weather
- Leaves collapse and die from tip
- Bulbs may rot (secondary bacteria)
- Spreads rapidly in cool (12-15°C), humid weather

Chemical Treatment:
1. Metalaxyl + Mancozeb (Ridomil Gold) — 400g per acre
   Apply preventatively every 7-10 days
2. Copper oxychloride — 500g per acre
3. Fosetyl-Al — 250g per acre

Prevention:
- Well-drained soil — raised beds
- Avoid overhead irrigation
- Crop rotation (3-4 years)
- Destroy infected crop debris

=========================================================================
PEST: Onion Thrips (Thrips tabaci) — "Pyaaz ki Makhi"
Category: PEST | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Silvery-white streaks on leaves
- Leaves turn brown and die from tip
- Severely affected plants have stunted bulbs
- Tiny black insects visible in leaf folds

Economic Threshold: 5-10 thrips per leaf

Chemical Treatment:
1. Spinosad (Tracer) — 60ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 600-800 per acre
2. Lambda-cyhalothrin — 200ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 400-600 per acre
3. Neem oil — 500ml per acre (mild infestations)

Prevention:
- Remove previous onion debris
- Intercrop with carrots or tomatoes
- Overhead irrigation suppresses thrips

CROP: Potato (aloo) | Season: Rabi (Oct-Jan) | Region: Punjab, KPK, Sindh

DISEASE: Late Blight of Potato (Phytophthora infestans) — "Aloo Jali"
Category: DISEASE | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Water-soaked lesions on leaves — expand rapidly in wet weather
- White fungal growth on leaf undersides
- Brown firm rot on tubers — internal reddish-brown discoloration
- Plants collapse within 3-5 days of infection in cool wet weather
- Storage: tubers rot (soft rot bacteria follow)

Chemical Control:
1. Metalaxyl + Mancozeb (Ridomil Gold) — 400g per acre
   Apply PREVENTATIVELY (before rain) — curative is too late
2. Chlorothalonil (Bravo) — 500ml per acre
3. Propineb — 500g per acre

Seed Treatment:
- Treat seed potatoes: Mancozeb 2.5g per liter water — dip 10 minutes
- Use certified disease-free seed only

Prevention:
- Resistant varieties: Desiree, Sante, Kuroda (check local availability)
- Destroy volunteer potatoes and cull piles
- High ridges for earthing (helps prevent tuber infection)
- Harvest in dry weather
- Store in cool (>10°C) dark place

=========================================================================
DISEASE: Potato Scab (Streptomyces scabies)
Category: DISEASE | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Brown corky scabs/lesions on tuber surface
- Raised or pitted lesions — cosmetic damage
- Worse in dry, alkaline soils

Chemical:
- Seed treatment with Mancozeb
- Soil: maintain pH 5.5-6.0 (acidic soils suppress scab)

=========================================================================
DISEASE: Potato Blackleg (Pectobacterium atrosepticum)
Category: DISEASE | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Black rot starting at stem base
- Plants wilt, yellow, and die
- Tubers rot — foul smell
- ~90% of blackleg in Pakistan caused by Pectobacterium atrosepticum

Chemical:
- NO cure — remove and destroy infected plants
- Use certified disease-free seed
- Avoid overwatering in heavy soils
- Crop rotation 3-4 years
- Cut seed potatoes with clean knife

FERTILIZER: Potato Nutrient Schedule
Category: FERTILIZER | Urgency: PLANNED

NPK per acre:
- Nitrogen: 35-45 kg/acre (75-100 kg Urea)
- Phosphorus: 20-25 kg/acre (125-155 kg SSP)
- Potash: 25-30 kg/acre (40-50 kg MOP) — Potato is heavy K user

Application:
1. Basal (at sowing): Full P + Full K + 1/3 N
2. First earthing (30-35 days): 1/3 N
3. Second earthing (50-55 days): 1/3 N

IRRIGATION: Potato
- 4-6 irrigations
- Critical: tuber initiation stage (30-50 days)
- Stop irrigation 10-14 days before harvest (better storage)
- Sandy soils: irrigate every 5-7 days
- Heavy soils: every 10-12 days
