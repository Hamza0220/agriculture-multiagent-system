DISEASE: Rice Blast (Magnaporthe oryzae) — "Chawal ki Jali"
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: Punjab (central), Sindh | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
**Leaf Blast:** Diamond-shaped lesions with gray center and brown edges on leaves
**Neck Blast:** Gray-brown lesions on panicle neck — causes grain sterility
**Node Blast:** Rotting/blackening at stem nodes
**Yield Loss:** Up to 50% in severe neck blast

Conditions Favoring Disease:
- High humidity (>90%) with intermittent rain
- Moderate temperatures (25-28°C)
- Excessive nitrogen fertilizer
- Dense canopy
- Night temperatures below 20°C

Chemical Treatment:
1. Tricyclazole (Beam) — 400ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 600-900 per acre
   Apply at disease appearance or before booting stage
   Second spray at 50% flowering if needed
   Local brands: Beam 75WP (Dow AgroSciences)

2. Carbendazim — 200g per acre
   Cost: Rs. 400-600 per acre

3. Isoprothiolane (Fuji-One) — 400ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 800-1200 per acre

4. Propiconazole (Tilt) — 200ml per acre
   Note: Less effective than Tricyclazole for rice blast

Application:
- Spray when disease reaches 5% severity on upper leaves
- High volume spray (100L water per acre)
- Early morning application preferred

Prevention:
- Resistant varieties: Super Basmati (moderately), KSK-133, Kissan Basmati
- Balanced nitrogen — split applications (don't dump all at once)
- Avoid continuous rice-rice rotation
- Drain field for 2-3 days if leaf blast is severe
- Seed treatment: Carbendazim 2g/kg seed

DISEASE: Bacterial Leaf Blight (Xanthomonas oryzae) — "Bacterical Jhulsao"
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: Punjab (irrigated areas) | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Water-soaked yellow to white streaks along leaf veins
- Lesions start from leaf tips/margins, extend downward
- Dried leaf tips (look like burnt)
- Bacterial ooze (yellow droplets) on young lesions in early morning
- In severe cases: complete leaf drying, yield loss 20-40%

Conditions Favoring Disease:
- High humidity with rain and wind
- Excessive nitrogen fertilization
- Flood-irrigated fields with standing water
- Wounding from wind, insects, or implements

Chemical Treatment:
1. Streptocycline (Streptomycin + Tetracycline) — 15g per acre
   Cost: Rs. 500-700 per acre
   Apply at first appearance of symptoms
   Repeat after 10 days if weather remains favorable
   
2. Copper oxychloride — 500g per acre
   Cost: Rs. 300-500 per acre

3. Copper hydroxide (Kocide) — 300g per acre

Prevention:
- Resistant varieties available (check local recommendations)
- Reduce nitrogen application during active infection
- Drain field before spraying for better chemical contact
- Avoid walking through flooded field when diseased (spreads bacteria)
- Field sanitation: remove infected straw after harvest
- Copper-based sprays PREVENTATIVE, not curative — apply before disease appears in high-risk areas

=========================================================================
DISEASE: Sheath Blight (Rhizoctonia solani) — "Ganwari" 
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: All rice regions | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Greenish-gray water-soaked lesions on leaf sheaths near waterline
- Lesions enlarge, become oval/elliptical with gray center and brown border
- Lesions coalesce, moving up the plant
- Infected sheaths rot, leaves die
- "Banded" appearance on sheaths
- Yield loss: 10-30% in severe cases

Conditions Favoring Disease:
- Dense canopy with high humidity
- High nitrogen, dense planting
- Continuous rice-wheat rotation (both crops are hosts)
- Standing water in fields

Chemical Treatment:
1. Hexaconazole (Contaf) — 250ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 400-600 per acre
2. Carbendazim (Bavistin) — 200g per acre  
3. Validamycin — 200ml per acre
   Cost: Yes, effective but less available in Pakistan [VERIFY WITH LOCAL EXPERT]

Prevention:
- Optimum spacing: plant at 20x20cm or 22x22cm
- Balanced nitrogen — avoid excess
- Drain field for 4-5 days when disease appears
- Avoid continuous rice-wheat

=========================================================================
DISEASE: Rice Smut / False Smut (Ustilaginoidea virens) — "Pili Jali"
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: Punjab | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Individual rice grains replaced by large yellow-green fungal balls
- Balls turn orange-green to black when mature
- Only some spikelets per panicle affected
- Balls are bigger than rice grains
- More noticeable at harvest

Chemical Treatment:
- Propiconazole (Tilt) 200ml per acre at booting stage (PREVENTATIVE)
- Carbendazim 200g per acre at booting

Prevention:
- Avoid excessive nitrogen at panicle initiation
- Remove and destroy infected grains (NOT eaten or fed to animals — toxic)

=========================================================================
PEST: Rice Stem Borer (Scirpophaga incertulas) — "Tana Sundi" / "Gandhi"
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: PEST | Season: Kharif
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- **Dead heart:** Central leaf dries up during vegetative stage (larva bores stem)
- **White ear head:** Panicle dries prematurely (larva bores at grain stage)
- Wilting plants, yellowing
- Small holes at stem base with frass (excreta)
- Eggs laid on leaf tips in masses covered with brown hairs

Threshold: 10% dead hearts in vegetative stage, 5% white ears at heading

Chemical Treatment:
1. Cartap hydrochloride — 400g per acre
   Cost: Rs. 500-700 per acre
2. Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen) — 60ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 800-1000 per acre — very effective
3. Fipronil — 400ml per acre

Prevention:
- Deep plowing after harvest destroys overwintering larvae
- Timely transplanting (before July 15 in Punjab)
- Use light traps for monitoring adult moths
- Avoid split-dose nitrogen (attracts more borers when spread out)

=========================================================================
PEST: Rice Leaf Folder (Cnaphalocrocis medinalis) — "Patti Larhai"  
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: PEST | Season: Kharif
Region: All regions | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Leaves folded and tied together with silk threads
- Green tissue scraped inside folded leaf — white/transparent strips
- Yield loss from reduced photosynthesis
- Damage more serious from panicle initiation through flowering

Threshold: 10-15% damaged leaves before booting

Chemical Treatment:
1. Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen) — 60ml per acre
2. Lambda-cyhalothrin — 150ml per acre
3. Spinosad (Tracer) — 60ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 600-800 per acre

Prevention:
- Natural enemies (spiders, wasps) effective — avoid early sprays
- Resistant varieties available [VERIFY WITH LOCAL EXPERT]

=========================================================================
PEST: Brown Planthopper (Nilaparvata lugens) — "Boori Sundi"  
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: PEST | Season: Kharif
Region: Punjab (central) | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Large numbers of small brown insects at base of plant near waterline
- Leaves turn yellow-orange then brown ("hopper burn") in circular patches
- Plant dries up and dies — patches resemble drought damage
- Insects fly up when plant is shaken
- Also transmits viral diseases (grassy stunt, ragged stunt)

Threshold: 10-15 hoppers per hill (tiller)

Chemical Treatment:
1. Imidacloprid (Confidor) — 100ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 600-800 per acre
   Apply only when threshold crossed (overuse creates resistance)
   
2. Pymetrozine — 200g per acre
3. Buprofezin — 300ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 500-700 per acre

CRITICAL: BPH has developed resistance to many insecticides. Use as part of
integrated pest management. Avoid pyrethroids on BPH (makes it worse).

Prevention:
- Avoid dense planting — maintain spacing
- Don't over-fertilize with nitrogen
- Keep field drained for 3-4 days when hoppers detected
- Natural enemies: spiders, mirid bugs — conserve them
- Use Trichoderma-treated nursery

FERTILIZER: Rice Nutrient Schedule
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: FERTILIZER | Season: Kharif
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: PLANNED

Recommended NPK per acre (Basmati varieties):
- Nitrogen: 35-50 kg/acre (75-110 kg Urea)
- Phosphorus: 12-20 kg/acre (75-125 kg SSP)
- Potash: 0-12 kg/acre (0-20 kg MOP) — soil test recommended
- Zinc: 10-15 kg Zinc Sulfate per acre — CRITICAL for Basmati
- Sulphur: 5-10 kg per acre on light soils

Application Timing:
1. Basal (at last puddling): Full P + Full K + half Zn + 1/3 N
   — Incorporate into soil before transplanting
2. Top-dressing 1 (15-20 days after transplanting): 1/3 N + half Zn
   — At active tillering stage
3. Top-dressing 2 (35-40 days DAT): 1/3 N
   — At panicle initiation stage

Important Facts:
- Basmati rice needs LESS nitrogen than coarse varieties (lodges easily)
- Zinc deficiency causes "khaira" disease — leaves turn yellow-brown, stunting
- Apply Zinc Sulfate at 10-15kg/acre — MUST for Basmati
- Silicon: rice benefits from — use rice husk ash where available
- Never dump all nitrogen at once — split application critical for disease management

IRRIGATION: Rice Water Management
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: IRRIGATION | Season: Kharif
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: PLANNED

Critical: Rice is NOT a "water waster" — it needs standing water but can be managed

Water Requirements by Stage:
1. Nursery (40-45 days): Keep moist 2-3cm standing water after 7 days
2. Transplanting to 15 DAT: 2-3cm standing water (helps rooting)
3. Tillering (15-35 DAT): 5-7cm standing water
4. Panicle Initiation (35-55 DAT): 10-15cm standing water — MOST CRITICAL STAGE
5. Flowering/Grain filling (55-85 DAT): 5-7cm standing water
6. Dough stage (85-105 DAT): Keep moist — drain gradually
7. Maturity (105-115 DAT): Drain completely — harvest 7-10 days after draining

Irrigation Intervals (sandy loam, non-Basmati):
- Every 2-3 days in hot weather
- Every 4-5 days in moderate weather
- Every 3-4 days during strong winds

Water Conservation Tips:
- Laser leveling before puddling saves 25-30% water
- Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD) — irrigate when water level drops 15cm below soil
  — AWD reduces water by 25% without yield loss
  — Do NOT use AWD at flowering stage (critical period)
- Short-duration varieties use less water
- Direct Seeded Rice (DSR) uses 30-50% less water than transplanting

STORAGE: Rice Post-Harvest Storage
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: STORAGE | Season: Kharif
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: PLANNED

- Harvest at 20-22% moisture (paddy)
- Thresh within 24 hours
- Dry paddy to 14% moisture within 48 hours
- Store at <12% moisture for long-term (6+ months)
- Sun dry: spread in thin layer, turn every 30 minutes
- Store in clean gunny bags on wooden pallets (not on floor)
- Use Phostoxin tablet: 1 per 100kg paddy
- Check monthly for insects and moisture

=========================================================================
HARVESTING: Rice Harvest Guide  
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: HARVESTING | Season: Kharif
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: THIS SEASON

- Harvest when 80% grains turn golden yellow
- Panicle neck becomes pale yellow
- Grains are hard and barely leave dent when bitten
- Manual: cut with sickles, bundle, sun dry 3-4 days
- Combine harvester: direct harvesting cost Rs. 2000-3000 per acre
- Threshing: Rs. 400-500 per acre (machine)
- Drying: spread paddy 2 inches, turn every hour, 2-3 sunny days
- Recent market price (2025): Basmati Rs. 4000-5000 per 40kg (paddy)
- Coarse varieties (IRRI): Rs. 2000-2800 per 40kg (paddy)

=========================================================================
SOIL: Rice Soil Requirements
=========================================================================
Crop: rice (chawal) | Category: SOIL | Season: Kharif
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: PLANNED

- Clay loam to silty clay ideal (holds water)
- Heavy clay: Basmati grows best
- Sandy loam: needs more water and fertilizer
- pH: 5.5-7.0
- Good soil: rice grows well in slightly acidic soils
- Saline/sodic soils: NOT suitable for rice (salt-sensitive)
- Soil should hold 5-10cm standing water percolation loss of 5-10mm/day
