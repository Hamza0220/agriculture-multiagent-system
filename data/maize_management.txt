DISEASE: Maize Leaf Blight (Helminthosporium maydis) — "Pati Jali"
=========================================================================
Crop: maize (makki) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: All maize areas | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Small water-soaked spots on leaves
- Spots enlarge into elongated gray-green to tan lesions
- Lesions coalesce — large areas of leaf die
- Lower leaves affected first, then upward
- Severe: complete leaf blight, reduced grain filling

Chemical Treatment:
1. Mancozeb (Dithane M-45) — 500g per acre in 100L water
   Cost: Rs. 300-500 per acre
2. Propiconazole (Tilt) — 200ml per acre
3. Carbendazim — 200g per acre

Prevention:
- Resistant varieties: hybrid varieties are more resistant than open-pollinated
- Crop rotation
- Clean field of previous crop debris

PEST: Fall Armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda) — "Phal Sundi"
=========================================================================
Crop: maize (makki) | Category: PEST | Season: Kharif
Region: All regions (invasive, first reported in Pakistan 2019) | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Irregular holes/chewing damage on young leaves (whorl feeding)
- Frass (excrement) visible in leaf whorl
- Window-pane damage on leaves (young larvae scrape surface)
- Larvae drop to ground at maturity
- Bores into ear — direct grain damage
- Scattered damage pattern (moths lay eggs in batches)

Larva identification:
- Inverted "Y" shape on head
- Four dark spots in trapezoid on last body segment
- Colors vary: green, brown, black

Chemical Control (apply to whorl — where larvae hide):
1. Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen) — 60ml per acre in 100L water
   Pour into leaf whorl, NOT just sprayed over top
   Cost: Rs. 800-1000 per acre
   BEST option — one application can give 2-3 weeks control

2. Spinosad (Tracer) — 60ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 600-800 per acre
   Alternative for organic farming

3. Lambda-cyhalothrin — 200ml per acre
   Cost: Rs. 400-600
   Moderate effectiveness — use as last resort

4. Emamectin benzoate — 80g per acre
   Cost: Rs. 500-700 per acre

Biological Control:
- Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) — 500ml per acre (young larvae only)
- Beauveria bassiana — 250g per acre — fungal biocontrol
- Natural predators: wasps (Trichogramma), ants, spiders
- Neem oil — 500ml per acre — moderate repellent effect (NOT curative)

Prevention:
- Early planting: avoid peak pest season
- Intercrop with cowpea or beans (push-pull technology)
- Pheromone traps for monitoring — 3-4 per acre from emergence
- Deep summer plowing destroys pupae

CRITICAL: Fall Armyworm is an invasive pest in Pakistan since 2019.
Report severe infestations to local Agriculture Department.

DISEASE: Maize Stem Borer — "Tana Sundi"
=========================================================================
Crop: maize (makki) | Category: PEST | Season: Kharif
Region: Punjab, KPK | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- "Shot hole" damage in leaves (young larvae feeding)
- Dead heart in whorl stage
- Stem tunneling with frass at entry holes
- Broken stalks at nodes
- Poor ear development

Chemical Control:
1. Carbofuran (Furadan) — 5-8kg per acre
   Apply in whorl at first sign
2. Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen) — 60ml per acre (same FAW schedule covers stem borer)
3. Cartap hydrochloride — 400g per acre

FERTILIZER: Maize Nutrient Schedule
=========================================================================
Crop: maize (makki) | Category: FERTILIZER | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: PLANNED

NPK per acre:
- Nitrogen: 50-65 kg/acre (110-140 kg Urea)
- Phosphorus: 15-20 kg/acre (95-125 kg SSP)
- Potash: 10-15 kg/acre (17-25 kg MOP)
- Zinc: 5-8 kg Zinc Sulfate

Application:
1. Basal: Full P + Full K + 1/3 N at sowing
2. First top (30-35 days): 1/3 N — at knee-high stage
3. Second top (50-55 days): 1/3 N — at tasseling

CRITICAL: Zn deficiency causes "white bud" — seedlings with white/yellow leaves
Zinc is MUST for maize in heavy soils and high pH conditions

=========================================================================
IRRIGATION: Maize Water Management
=========================================================================
Crop: maize (makki) | Category: IRRIGATION | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: PLANNED

Total: 4-6 irrigations

Critical stages:
1. 20-25 DAS (knee-high stage)
2. 35-40 DAS (tasseling) — MOST CRITICAL
3. 50-55 DAS (silking)
4. 65-70 DAS (grain filling)
5. 80-85 DAS (dough stage) — light irrigation if needed

Water stress at tasseling/silking: causes severe yield loss (40-60%)
Dry weather during grain filling is usually fine (drought-tolerant crop)
