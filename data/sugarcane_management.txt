IRRIGATION: Sugarcane Water Management
=========================================================================
Crop: sugarcane (ganna) | Category: IRRIGATION | Season: Kharif
Region: Punjab, Sindh | Urgency: PLANNED

Sugarcane is a heavy water user — needs 12-18 irrigations depending on soil

Stages:
1. Germination (0-60 days): Keep soil moist — irrigate every 7-10 days
   3-4 irrigations critical for proper tillering
2. Tillering (60-120 days): Moderate — irrigate every 10-12 days
   Water stress here reduces cane population
3. Grand growth (120-270 days): CRITICAL — irrigate every 8-12 days
   Most water-demanding stage — 8-10 irrigations
   Moisture stress = thin canes, low sugar
4. Maturity (270-330 days): Reduce gradually — stop 2-3 weeks before harvest
   Last irrigation: 20-30 days before harvest

Total: 15-20 irrigations for autumn crop, 12-15 for spring crop

Canal vs Tube Well:
- Canal water: more available in Sindh, supplements in Punjab
- Tube well: Punjab uses heavily — 3-4 hours per acre per irrigation
- Water requirement: 2000-3000mm per season (very high)

Conservation:
- Trash mulching (dried leaves left on field) saves 25% water
- Drip irrigation: trialed in Pakistan with 40% water saving [VERIFY WITH LOCAL EXPERT]
- Furrow irrigation is standard

FERTILIZER: Sugarcane Nutrient Schedule
=========================================================================
Crop: sugarcane (ganna) | Category: FERTILIZER | Season: Kharif
Region: All Pakistan | Urgency: PLANNED

Recommended NPK per acre:
- Nitrogen: 80-100 kg/acre (175-220 kg Urea) — HIGH user
- Phosphorus: 25-30 kg/acre (155-190 kg SSP)
- Potash: 20-25 kg/acre (35-40 kg MOP)
- Zinc: 8-10 kg Zinc Sulfate

Application:
1. Basal (at sowing): Full P + Full K + 1/4 N
   — Well-rotted FYM 8-10 tonnes/acre + DAP 2 bags + MOP 15kg
2. First top-dressing (45-60 days): 1/4 N
   — Urea 55kg — at tillering stage
3. Second top-dressing (90-120 days): 1/4 N  
   — Urea 55kg — at grand growth initiation
4. Third top-dressing (150-180 days): 1/4 N
   — Urea 55kg — at peak grand growth

Ratoon crop: apply 25-30% MORE nitrogen than plant crop (autumn)
Earthing-up: apply fertilizer before earthing-up, not after

PEST: Sugarcane Stem Borer — "Ganna Ki Sundi"
=========================================================================
Crop: sugarcane (ganna) | Category: PEST | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Top Borer (Scirpophaga excerptalis):
- Deadheart with parallel rows of holes on leaf
- Central leaf dries — "dead heart"

Stem Borer (Chilo infuscatellus):
- Small holes on stem with frass (excreta)  
- Stem breaks at nodes ("dead heart" and side shoots)

Root Borer (Emmalocera depressella):
- Plants wilt and die in patches
- Roots damaged, plant easily pulled

Chemical Treatment:
1. Carbofuran (Furadan) — 8kg per acre — at planting in furrow
   Cost: Rs. 500-700 per acre
   ALTERNATIVE: Fipronil — 4kg per acre
2. Chlorantraniliprole (Coragen) — 60ml per acre at 60-90 days

Biological Control:
- Trichogramma cards: 5-8 cards per acre every 2 weeks (3 releases)
- Release egg parasitoid Trichogramma chilonis
- Cost: Very low — Rs. 200-300 per acre per release

Prevention:
- Set light traps at field edges
- Remove and destroy infested shoots (dead hearts)
- Deep plowing after harvest
- Resistant varieties: CPF-248, HSF-240
- Avoid ratooning of infested fields

DISEASE: Red Rot (Colletotrichum falcatum) — "Surkh Saran"
=========================================================================
Crop: sugarcane (ganna) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: Punjab, Sindh | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Dull red discoloration inside cane — longitudinal red streaks
- Cane splits longitudinally
- Leaves dry from tip downward
- Alcohol-like fermentation smell from infected cane
- Can kill entire field in severe cases

Treatment (NO effective curative — prevention only):
1. Seed treatment: Carbendazim 2g/liter water — dip setts (cuttings) for 10 minutes
2. Hot water treatment: soak setts at 52°C for 30 minutes — kills fungus internally
3. Remove and destroy infected plants immediately (burn or deep bury)
4. Avoid growing sugarcane in same field for 3 years

Resistant Varieties (for Red Rot prone areas):
- CPF-248, CPF-247, CP-85-1491 [VERIFY WITH LOCAL EXPERT]

=========================================================================
DISEASE: Smut (Ustilago scitaminea) — "Khari"
=========================================================================
Crop: sugarcane (ganna) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Black whip-like structure emerges from top of stalk
- Whip covered with black dust (teliospores)
- Stunted, bushy growth — "grass-like" appearance
- Early infection = no cane formation

Chemical:
- Carbendazim seed treatment (same as red rot)
- Remove infected plants with whip intact (bag whip before cutting to avoid spore spread)

VARIETIES: Sugarcane Varieties for Pakistan
=========================================================================
Crop: sugarcane (ganna) | Category: VARIETIES | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: PLANNED

Punjab:
- CPF-248 — High sugar content, red rot tolerant
- CPF-247 — High yield, good ratooning
- HSF-240 — Early maturity, good sugar recovery
- SPF-234 — Good for heavy soils
- S-2003-US-633 — High yield

Sindh:
- BL-4 — Early maturing, high sugar
- NIA-98 — High yield
- PR-1000 — Mid-season, good ratoon
- BF-129 — Good for waterlogged areas

KPK:
- Mardan-93 — Local adaptation

Ratooning Tips:
- Best varieties for ratoon: CPF-248, HSF-240 (good regrowth)
- Ratoon with 25-30% more N
- Make stubble cut close to ground (2-3 inches)
- Gap filling within 15 days

HARVESTING: Sugarcane
=========================================================================
Crop: sugarcane (ganna) | Category: HARVESTING | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: THIS SEASON

- Harvest when sugar recovery peaks (November-April)
- Top visible internode filled (no pithiness)
- Brix meter reading: 18-20% or higher
- Manual: cut close to ground, remove tops, strip leaves
- Use sharp digger to uproot
- Transport to mill within 24-48 hours (quality deteriorates after cutting)
- Recent price (2025): Rs. 350-500 per 40kg (varies by mill agreement)
- Factory lead time: line up delivery with mill (avoid long queues)

=========================================================================
STORAGE: Sugarcane
=========================================================================
Crop: sugarcane (ganna) | Category: STORAGE | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: PLANNED

- DON'T store harvested cane in open for more than 48 hours (sugar inversion/loss)
- Keep stacked in shade, wet jute covering on top
- Spray with water if storing >24 hours
- Better to leave standing in field and harvest as needed
- Ratooning possible if fields are managed well immediately after harvest
