DISEASE: Anthracnose (Colletotrichum gloeosporioides) — "Kali Jali"
=========================================================================
Crop: mango (aam) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: All mango areas | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
On Leaves:
- Irregular black/brown spots on leaves
- Leaf tip blight and dieback
- Leaf drop in severe cases

On Flowers:
- Black spots on flowers and panicles
- Flowers drop without fruit set ("blossom blight")

On Fruit:
- Black sunken spots on fruit (can expand to large rotten areas)
- In storage: fruit rot develops after harvest
- Most damaging stage: flowering — can ruin entire crop

Conditions:
- High humidity and rainfall during flowering (Feb-March)
- Spreads by rain splash
- Worst in Sindh (Mirpurkhas area) during wet spring

Chemical Treatment (timing CRITICAL — start at flower emergence):
1. Nativo 75WG (Trifloxystrobin + Tebuconazole) — 100g per 100L water
   Efficacy: 92% control of anthracnose
   Cost: Rs. 1200-1600 per acre
   Apply at: Flower emergence, full bloom, fruit set (3 sprays)

2. Cabriotop 60WG (Metiram + Pyraclostrobin) — 200g per 100L water
   Efficacy: 85-90% control

3. Copper oxychloride — 500g per 100L water
   Cost: Rs. 400-600 per acre
   Apply at 10-15 day intervals during wet weather

4. Topsin M (Thiophanate methyl) — 200g per 100L water

Spray Schedule (for orchards with history of anthracnose):
  1st spray: At flower bud emergence (Feb 1-15)
  2nd spray: At full bloom (Feb 25 - Mar 5)
  3rd spray: At fruit set (pebble stage — Mar 15-25)
  4th spray: 2 weeks before harvest (Apr-May)

Post-Harvest Treatment:
- Hot water treatment: Dip fruit in 52°C water for 5 minutes
- Reduces post-harvest anthracnose significantly

Prevention:
- Prune dead/diseased branches after harvest
- Remove fallen leaves and fruit from orchard floor
- Maintain open canopy (pruning for air circulation)
- Avoid overhead irrigation during flowering
- Resistant varieties: Sindhri (moderately resistant), Chunsa (susceptible)

DISEASE: Powdery Mildew (Oidium mangiferae) — "Safed Phaphundi"
=========================================================================
Crop: mango (aam) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: All mango areas | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- White powdery coating on flowers, fruit, and young leaves
- Affected flowers drop (no fruit set)
- Young fruit — covered in white powder, cracks, and drops
- Leaves — white patches, curl, and drop
- Can cause 60-90% fruit loss in severe epidemics

Conditions:
- Moderate temperatures (20-25°C) with high humidity
- Dry weather with morning dew favors disease
- Most severe during flowering (Feb-March)
- Cultivars: Chunsa and Anwar Ratool highly susceptible

Chemical Treatment:
1. Nativo 75WG (Trifloxystrobin + Tebuconazole) — 100g per 100L water
   Efficacy: 90% control of powdery mildew
   Cost: Rs. 1200-1600 per acre

2. Topas 100EC (Penconazole) — 100ml per 100L water
   Efficacy: 90%
   Cost: Rs. 500-700 per acre

3. Vangard 25EC (Triadimenol) — 200ml per 100L water
   Efficacy: 92%

4. Sulfur-based (wettable sulfur) — 400g per 100L water
   Cost: Rs. 200-300 per acre
   Organic option — apply when temperatures below 30°C (can burn above 30°C)

5. Karathane (Dinocap) — 100ml per 100L water

Spray Schedule:
  1st spray: At flower panicle emergence (first sign of mildew)
  2nd spray: 10-14 days after first
  3rd spray: After fruit set

CRITICAL: Powdery mildew must be controlled BEFORE flowers open.
Post-infection sprays are less effective.

Prevention:
- Prune for open canopy
- Avoid dense planting
- Remove alternate hosts in surrounding area

DISEASE: Mango Malformation — "Aam ki Behoodgi"
=========================================================================
Crop: mango (aam) | Category: DISEASE | Season: Kharif
Region: All, especially Punjab (Multan) | Urgency: MODERATE

Symptoms:
- Dense cluster of short, thick shoots ("witches broom")
- Malformed flower panicles — flowers don't open or set fruit
- Vegetative malformation: excessive branching at shoot tip
- Floral malformation: 100% yield loss on affected panicles
- Cause: Fusarium mangiferae (fungus) + mite association

No effective chemical treatment once malformation visible

Management:
- Prune and burn malformed shoots in winter (December-January)
- Spray NAA (Naphthalene Acetic Acid) 200ppm at flower bud initiation
- Avoid use of severely infected wood for propagation
- Remove malformed panicles early (before they produce spores)
- Bio-control: Trichoderma harzianum soil application [VERIFY WITH LOCAL EXPERT]

PEST: Mango Mealy Bug (Drosicha mangiferae) — "Aam ki Sundi"
=========================================================================
Crop: mango (aam) | Category: PEST | Season: Kharif
Region: All mango areas | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Cottony white masses on branches and leaves
- Large clusters of insects sucking sap from tender shoots
- Honeydew on leaves — sooty mold follows
- Shoot dieback and leaf drop
- Fruit: covered with insects, unmarketable

Chemical Treatment:
1. Chlorpyrifos + Mineral oil — 500ml + 1 liter per acre
   Apply in December-January (egg stage)
   Cost: Rs. 600-800 per acre
2. Imidacloprid 17.8SL — 100ml per acre
   Apply when nymphs appear (February)
3. Banding: sticky bands/alkathene sheets around trunk — prevents nymphs climbing

Prevention:
- Orchard sanitation — remove weeds
- Remove egg masses from bark in winter
- Band tree trunks in December (sticky bands at 3-4 ft height)
- Spray lower trunk with chlorpyrifos

PEST: Mango Fruit Fly — "Aam ki Makhi"
=========================================================================
Crop: mango (aam) | Category: PEST | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: IMMEDIATE

Symptoms:
- Fruit has small puncture marks (egg-laying)
- Inside: soft, mushy area with maggots
- Fruit drops prematurely
- Post harvest: fruit rots quickly
- Cannot see infestation from outside until fruit is cut

Control:
1. Pheromone traps (methyl eugenol) — 10-15 per acre
   Attract and kill male fruit flies
   Cost: Very cost-effective, Rs. 200-300 per trap
2. Protein bait spray: Malathion 2ml + Protein hydrolysate 5ml per liter water
   Spot spray on 1-2 branches per tree every 7 days
3. Collect and destroy all fallen fruit (deep bury)
4. Field sanitation — do not leave infested fruit in orchard

FERTILIZER: Mango Nutrient Schedule
=========================================================================
Crop: mango (aam) | Category: FERTILIZER | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: PLANNED

Per tree (10+ year old tree):
- FYM: 40-50 kg per tree (post-harvest, July-August)
- Urea: 1.5-2 kg per tree
- SSP: 1.5-2 kg per tree
- SOP/MOP: 1-1.5 kg per tree

Timing:
- Post harvest (July-Aug): Full FYM + full P + full K + 1/2 N
- Pre-flowering (Jan-Feb): 1/2 N
- After fruit set (March-April): micronutrients (Zn, B, Fe) foliar spray

For Young Trees (1-3 years):
- 1/4 of adult dose, increasing gradually
- More frequent, lighter applications

Micronutrients (very important for mango):
- Zinc: 15g Boron + 15g Zinc per 10L water — foliar spray before flowering
- Boron deficiency causes fruit cracking and poor fruit set
- Spray 3 times: before flowering, at pea stage, at marble stage

IRRIGATION: Mango Water Management
=========================================================================
Crop: mango (aam) | Category: IRRIGATION | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: PLANNED

Critical stages:
1. Post-harvest (June-July): heavy irrigation after fertilizer application
2. Flower initiation (January-February): light irrigation
3. Fruit development (March-May): regular irrigation at 10-15 day intervals
4. Pre-harvest (May-June): reduce water for better sugar content and shelf life

Stop irrigation 2-3 weeks before harvest for CHUNSA variety — improves sweetness
Drip irrigation in mango is emerging but not widespread [VERIFY WITH LOCAL EXPERT]
Over-irrigation during flowering = excessive vegetative growth, less fruit set

HARVESTING: Mango Harvest Guide
=========================================================================
Crop: mango (aam) | Category: HARVESTING | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: THIS SEASON

- Harvest: May-September (variety dependent)
- Sindhri: May-June | Chunsa: June-July| Dasehri: July | Anwar Ratool: August
- Harvest in morning (fruit is cool, longer shelf life)
- Clip with 2-3 inches stem (sap burn from broken stem damages peel)
- Place gently in baskets — don't drop or pile
- Keep in shade immediately after picking
- Hot water treatment: 52°C for 5 min for anthracnose control (if export-bound)
- Recent export price: varies enormously by grade and destination
- Domestic market price season: Rs. 2000-6000 per 40kg (varies by month/cultivar)

=========================================================================
STORAGE: Mango
=========================================================================
Crop: mango (aam) | Category: STORAGE | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: PLANNED

- Don't store in plastic bags (suffocation leads to rotting)
- Keep in single layer, stem up, in ventilated crates
- Ripening at room temperature: 2-7 days
- Cold storage: 10-12°C (lower temp = chilling injury)
- Do NOT refrigerate green mangoes (won't ripen properly)
- Onion + mango in same storage room: ethylene gas from onion speeds mango ripening

VARIETIES: Mango Varieties for Pakistan
=========================================================================
Crop: mango (aam) | Category: VARIETIES | Season: Kharif
Region: All | Urgency: PLANNED

Punjab (Multan):
- Chunsa — King of mangoes, June-July, very sweet, best for export
- Dasehri — July, fiberless, excellent taste
- Anwar Ratool — August, late season, good for processing
- Langra — July-August, distinct taste (tart-sweet)
- Sindhri — May-June, early, popular export (actually Sindh but grown elsewhere too)

Sindh (Mirpurkhas, Hyderabad):
- Sindhri — Premier Sindh variety, May-June, large, golden
- Neelum — June-September, biennial bearing
- Sunhara — Improved variety from Sindhri

KPK:
- Local varieties — smaller, unique taste
